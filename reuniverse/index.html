Three core mechanisms

Reuniverse rests on three rules that everything else falls out of.

1

Random, permanent identity

When you publish an idea, it is hashed and assigned a real celestial body from NASA's Exoplanet Archive or JPL's Small-Body Database. You might land on Kepler-186f, or on asteroid (153814) 2001 WN5. The body is permanent — civilization is not determined by where you start.

2

Star-driven civilization

Each public Star advances your planet through nine stages, from Pre-life to Type III. Even an asteroid can become a galaxy-scale civilization with enough collective attention. Attention here is not extracted — it is the resource that builds civilizations.

3

Workspace = real workshop

Click into any planet and you find a ZenHub-style workspace: kanban, epochs, roadmap, burndown. Issues sync two-way with a bound GitHub repo. A planet is both a social object and an actual workplace.

An open-source cosmos, by design

Every planet here is permanently public, permanently attributed, and permanently free to fork. Federation across instances is built on ActivityPub — one universe, many implementations.

If you want privacy, self-host. The code, the protocol, and the celestial catalogs are all open.
