Concept map / protocol sketch

The concept

Reuniverse is a public universe of open-source ideas. Every planet here is permanently public, permanently attributed, permanently free to fork. Civilization grows from public attention, not from the physical body it's born on.

You write down an idea. It gets a real celestial body for life. Other people can give it a Star, which advances its civilization through nine stages. Inside the planet is a real workspace: kanban, epochs, GitHub two-way sync. Planets that drift semantically close to each other can collide — and a collision proposal is generated by an LLM and offered back to both authors.

The platform is fully open source (AGPLv3) and federates across instances via ActivityPub.

Body assignment

When you publish an idea, the system hashes its content and uses the hash to draw a real celestial body from a weighted random pool:

  • 70% — asteroid (from JPL's Small-Body Database, 1.4M+ entries)
  • 25% — confirmed exoplanet (NASA Exoplanet Archive, 5,800+)
  • 4% — comet
  • 1% — moon

Most ideas land on a small rock, because most ideas at birth are marginal — that's an honest reflection of reality. Once assigned, the body is permanent. Even a Type III civilization keeps its asteroid identity. Civilization is not determined by where you start.

Nine civilization stages

Star count drives stage advancement. Thresholds grow roughly 5x per stage, so the top is rare. A Type III planet is a platform-level legendary idea.

    Pre-life

    Type III

ai4math #4 — s-invariant from a knot diagram

Train a neural network to look directly at a knot diagram and predict the Rasmussen s-invariant, targeting the 20-30 crossing regime where standard Khovanov homology computations are infeasible. Recent work (Davies-DeepMind 2021; the 2025 "Data-Driven Perspectives on Knot Invariants" paper covering ~7.5M non-alternating knots up to 17 crossings) shows neural nets can learn topological invariants from braid words and PD codes — but going beyond 17 crossings is still open territory.

DAW + Score Therapy — hum to symbolic, AI-arranged

Hum two bars. The system transcribes you to symbolic notation (in the lineage of Klangio, Songscription, NeuralNote), then suggests next moves, picks a track, swaps timbres. Beyond a composing tool, the project explores it as a music-therapy modality: existing therapy work centers on real instruments and singing — computer-music production with humming as the entry point is largely untested. Patients who can't physically play an instrument get a path in.

Bridge AI Tutor — bidding, declarer, defense

An English-speaking conversational tutor for absolute beginners. Teaches the bidding system end-to-end, then declarer play, then defense — the three pieces NukkAI's headline 2022 win explicitly skipped (Nook only played declarer). When a convention or bidding system the student hasn't seen comes up, the tutor searches the web mid-lesson and brings it back.

Philosophy

Three anchors, one slogan: we came from the same dust; we're building it back.

Panpsychism / Cosmopsychism

From Strawson, Annaka Harris, and Roelofs: consciousness as a fundamental property of the universe, not an emergent fluke of carbon chemistry. Reuniverse takes the literal corollary — if consciousness is shared substrate, an idea on a small rock and an idea on a habitable exoplanet have the same metaphysical floor. What differs is attention.

Wheeler's "it from bit"

Each Star is a binary observation answer. A planet's civilization stage is the cumulative collapse of those answers. Physics is metaphor here, but the metaphor is honest: in this system, existence really is reinforced by being observed.

Attention economy, reversed

In the dominant model attention is a resource extracted from users. In Reuniverse attention is the resource that builds a civilization. You don't pay attention to the platform — the platform is what your collective attention builds.
