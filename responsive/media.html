A Song and a Road Trip

Theory is abstract; feeling is not. This page pairs Choice Potential Theory with two personal pieces of media — an original song I wrote called Snow Dreaming, and a short California travel video from my own trip. Neither is a literal illustration of the theory; they are here as reminders that the "degree" this site describes is something lived, not just reasoned about.

Audio — Snow Dreaming (Original Song)

Snow Dreaming is a song I wrote and produced myself. The slow harmonic drift was my attempt to capture the quiet, suspended feeling of sitting right at the edge of what you can hold — the same place Choice Potential Theory locates meaningful decision-making. This is the full version, about four minutes long.

Video — California Road Trip

A short travel video from my own trip through California — coastline, highway, the kind of open light that makes you feel like the next turn is the boundary you have been approaching. Filmed and edited by me, encoded as H.264 MP4 at 720p so it streams well across devices.

Media Notes

  • Audio — original song by Yixia Yu; MP3 with M4A fallback for broader browser support
  • Video — personal travel footage; H.264 MP4 at 1280×720, faststart enabled for progressive streaming
  • Controls — native HTML5 controls attribute, no external player required
  • Autoplay — disabled on purpose; visitors choose when to start

Why Include Media?

Decision theory is often presented as cold arithmetic. A song I wrote and a road I drove are meant to remind the reader that "degree" is a felt boundary — heard, seen, and moved through as much as reasoned about.
